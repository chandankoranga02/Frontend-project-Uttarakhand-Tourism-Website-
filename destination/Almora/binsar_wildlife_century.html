Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary

Dense oak forests, Himalayan peaks, and over 200 bird species — Binsar is one of Kumaon’s most serene and scenic wildlife sanctuaries.

Overview

Located about 30 km from Almora, Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary is spread across dense oak, rhododendron, and pine forests in the middle Himalayas. The sanctuary sits at an altitude of 2,000 to 2,500 meters and is known for its peaceful trails, rich biodiversity, and panoramic views of peaks like Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Panchachuli.

It’s a paradise for bird watchers and nature enthusiasts. The quiet atmosphere, cool breeze, and thick green canopy make it one of Uttarakhand’s most tranquil spots. Inside the sanctuary, there’s also the famous Zero Point, which offers a 360° view of the snow-capped ranges.

Highlights

  • Over 200 species of birds and various wildlife including barking deer and leopards
  • Dense oak, rhododendron, and pine forests — ideal for short treks
  • Zero Point viewpoint for panoramic Himalayan views
  • Eco-friendly lodges and KMVN guest houses inside the sanctuary
  • Sunrise and sunset views from Binsar ridge are breathtaking
Visitor Tips:

Entry closes by evening — plan early morning visits. Carry binoculars for bird watching. The roads inside are narrow; drive carefully. Warm clothes recommended even in summer.

Nearest Airport: Pantnagar (145 km)

Entry Fee
