Chitai — Golu Devta Temple

Famous hill-temple dedicated to Golu Devta — known for the bell-covered shrine and thousands of legal-style wish petitions.

Spiritual
Culture
Almora

Overview

Chitai (near Almora) hosts the revered Golu Devta Temple — a unique local deity worship where devotees tie bells, submit handwritten petitions, and believe in quick justice and wish-fulfillment. The temple sits on a small hill, offering views of the surrounding Kumaon hills and terraced fields.

The atmosphere is rustic and lively: brass bells of all sizes hang from the trees and roof, paper petitions flutter around, and local priests (pujaris) often mediate ritual offerings. The idol represents Golu Devta (a folk incarnation of Shiva), and the temple blends tribal-cum-Hindu traditions.

Chitai is a short drive from Almora town and works well as a half-day cultural stop combined with other Almora attractions like Bright End Corner and Kasar Devi.

Highlights

  • Thousands of bells suspended across the complex
  • Devotees submit handwritten petitions (often seeking justice or boons)
  • Local festivals and vibrant rural rituals
  • Short, accessible hilltop shrine with scenic views
  • Strong cultural flavour — great for photos and anthropological interest
Quick tips:

Respect local customs — don’t remove or play with bells. Carry small offerings and cash for priests. Peak crowd: mornings and festival days. Avoid loud behaviour; photography is allowed but be polite during rituals.

Nearest Airport: Pantnagar (~120 km)
