Lakhudiyar Cave Paintings

Rock art gallery on limestone shelters — twin sites with prehistoric petroglyphs and cultural inscriptions near Almora.

Overview

Lakhudiyar (literally "one lakh caverns") is a group of rock shelters near the banks of the Suyal/Kosi River close to Almora. The site is famous for prehistoric petroglyphs and pictographs — human figures, animals, hunting scenes and abstract symbols — some of which date back several thousand years.

The paintings and carvings appear across two main clusters of caves and exposed rock faces. Scholars value Lakhudiyar for its insight into prehistoric life in the Kumaon hills and for the continuity of local folk motifs found in later cultural traditions.

Visiting Lakhudiyar gives you a compact combination of light trekking, river views, and a dive into ancient human expression — a must for anyone interested in archaeology and anthropology.

Highlights

  • Prehistoric petroglyphs and pictographs (human figures, animals, hunting scenes)
  • Two main rock-shelter clusters with accessible panels
  • Great spot for history buffs, students and photographers
  • Combine with a short riverside walk for dramatic photos
  • Interpretive signs at the main shelter (where maintained)
Visitor tips:

Do not touch or trace the paintings — oils from skin damage ancient pigments. Carry a hat, water and a camera with a zoom lens for detailed shots. Visit early morning to avoid harsh sunlight on faded panels. Respect protective ropes and signage.

Nearby & Combine

Close to Almora — easy half-day trip. Combine with Bright End Corner, Kasar Devi, or the Binsar/landscape circuit for a full-day cultural route.

Quick Facts

Avg time: 1–2 hours (depending on interest)

Access: Short walk from parking area; uneven ground

Best Season: Mar–Jun, Sep–Nov

How to Reach

From Almora: ~10–15 km — hire taxi or local transport to the Lakhudiyar parking; short walk (10–20 mins) to shelters.

Nearest Railhead: Kathgodam (~95 km)

Nearest Airport: Pantnagar (~120 km)

Facilities

  • Small parking area and local tea stalls
  • No major commercial facilities — carry essentials
  • Guides available locally for a small fee
