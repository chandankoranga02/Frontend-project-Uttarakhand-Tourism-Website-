Deer Park, Almora

Peaceful forest zone near Almora — home to deer, birds, and serene walking trails amidst pine and oak forests.

About Deer Park

Located just 3 km from Almora’s main town, Deer Park (also known as Mrig Vihar) is a refreshing green escape for nature lovers. The park is nestled among dense pine and oak trees and serves as a small wildlife reserve where visitors can see herds of deer, peacocks, and a variety of mountain birds. It’s a peaceful retreat ideal for evening walks, photography, or simply soaking in the calm Kumaoni landscape.

Travel Information

Nearest Town: Almora (3 km)

Nearest Bus Stand: Almora ISBT

Best Time to Visit: March to June and September to November

Average Duration: 1–2 hours

Entry Fee: Free

Highlights

  • Home to deer, monkeys, and various bird species
  • Calm nature trails for walking and relaxation
  • Panoramic view of the Himalayan foothills
  • Excellent photography spot during sunrise and sunset

Nearby Attractions

  • Bright End Corner (2 km)
  • Kasar Devi Temple (8 km)
  • Chitai Golu Devta Temple (6 km)

Tips for Visitors

Visit early morning or before sunset for the best wildlife sightings. Carry water, wear comfortable shoes, and avoid littering. The park is ideal for quiet nature walks — not commercial tourism.
