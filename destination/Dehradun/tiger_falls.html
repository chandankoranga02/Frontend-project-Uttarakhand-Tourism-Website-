Tiger Falls — Chakrata (Dehradun District)

A high, scenic cascade set in oak–rhododendron forests — a rewarding short trek and a cold plunge in summer.

Waterfall
Short trek
Swimming allowed (seasonal)

Overview

Tiger Falls is one of the most impressive waterfalls near Dehradun, located in the Chakrata region. The fall plunges dramatically through a rocky gorge framed by oak and rhododendron forests — the route to the base is scenic and offers multiple viewpoints. Depending on where you start, the approach involves a short drive from Chakrata followed by a trek of roughly 1–5 km through forest trails.

The waterfall is popular in summer for bathing and for photographers chasing long-exposure shots. In winter and immediately after heavy monsoon, access can be trickier: the trail grows slippery and water levels change. There are few commercial facilities at the site itself — small local stalls sell tea and snacks at the parking/entry point, but carry water and basic supplies for the hike.

Expect a mix of day-trippers, local families and adventure groups. Vehicles can come fairly close, but most visitors finish with a short walk to reach the viewpoint or the pool. Respect local signage — some areas are not safe for swimming because of undercurrents and submerged rocks.

Highlights

  • Impressive vertical drop (noted sources record ~312 ft / ~95 m) with a rocky plunge pool.
  • Scenic oak & rhododendron forest approach — great for short treks and nature photo-ops.
  • Good summer swimming spots — but check safety and local guidance before entering water.
  • Combine with Chakrata town sights for an overnight or day-long trip from Dehradun.
Quick tips:

Road to Chakrata is winding — drive carefully. Wear sturdy shoes; trail can be slippery after rains. Carry water and a light snack; mobile network may be patchy. Avoid visiting right after heavy monsoon or during storms. If you plan to swim, check locals for safe spots and avoid going alone.

Combine With

Tiger Falls is best combined with a Chakrata stop — include Deoban viewpoint, Budher Caves (Lal Tibba) or a short village walk to make a full-day or overnight nature trip from Dehradun.

Quick Facts

Distance from Dehradun: ≈ 85–100 km by road (varies by route).

Distance from Chakrata: ≈ 15–20 km (some routes require a 1–5 km trek).

Typical hours: Sunrise–Sunset (visit during daylight).

Suggested visit length: 2–4 hours (including trek and photos).

How to Reach

From Dehradun: Drive via NH7/Chakrata road — travel time ~2–3 hrs depending on traffic and route.

From Chakrata town: Local jeeps/taxis can take you close; final stretch often requires a short trek (1–5 km) on forest trails.

Parking: Limited parking at the trailhead/entry; small fees may apply in busy season.

Timings & Fees

  • Entry: Many sources list no formal statewide entry — local parking/visitor fees (small) may be charged at gate in peak season.
  • Opening: Open during daylight; avoid visiting after dark for safety.
  • Note: small local operators sometimes charge for parking/guide — carry cash.

Nearby Stays

  • Chakrata guesthouses — local, cosy options for overnight stays.
  • Dehradun hotels — if coming as a day trip from the city, stay in Dehradun and start early.
  • For trekking-style visits, consider homestays in Chakrata to get local guidance and early starts.

Cafes & Snacks

  • Local tea stalls — near parking and small shops on the Chakrata approach.
  • Chakrata town cafés — better refreshments before/after the trip.

Safety & Environment

Trail can be slippery after rains; avoid going alone and don’t venture into fast currents. Respect local flora and do not litter. Mobile coverage may be patchy — share your plan with someone before heading out.
