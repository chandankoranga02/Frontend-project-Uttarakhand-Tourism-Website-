Overview

The Forest Research Institute (FRI) in Dehradun is a sprawling campus of red-brick colonial architecture set inside well-manicured lawns and dense tree cover. Established as a premier centre for forestry research and training, the impressive Neo-classical building houses a botanical museum, herbarium and research departments — but it’s the grand colonnades, domes and long arched corridors that draw visitors and photographers.

Visitors come for more than exhibits: long shaded walks beneath mature trees, heritage-style architecture that looks good in every season, and the small on-site museums that explain India's forest biodiversity. Weekends are lively with families, photography students and guided school groups. The campus is large and flat — easy to explore on foot — with benches, lawns and many quiet corners for a picnic or a long read under a tree.

FRI also hosts seasonal events, botanical displays and occasional workshops. The museums inside the building (museum, herbarium and institute exhibits) often require a small additional ticket. While the core campus remains open to visitors, parts of FRI are active research areas — please follow signboards and avoid restricted zones.

Highlights

  • Iconic red-brick colonial architecture and long arched corridors ideal for photography.
  • Botanical Museum with forestry exhibits and taxidermy displays (separate small fee).
  • Large shady grounds — good for family picnics and relaxed walks.
  • Near several other Dehradun attractions — easy to combine into a half/full day.
Quick tips:

FRI opens around 9:00 AM — visit early to avoid midday crowds and harsh light. Wear comfortable shoes — grounds are extensive. Museum entries are usually charged separately (nominal). Respect "research area" signs — some labs and archives are closed to public. Best months: Oct–Mar for crisp skies and pleasant walking.

Quick Facts

Typical hours: 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM (public visiting hours)

Avg time to cover: 1.5–3 hours (including museum)

Entry fee: Nominal (campus entry often ₹20–₹40; museum tickets separate ₹40–₹60).

Best time to visit: Oct–Mar (pleasant weather)

How to Reach

From Dehradun Railway Station: ~4–7 km (taxi/auto 15–25 mins depending on traffic).

From ISBT: Buses and autos available; approx 20–30 mins by road.

Parking: Small paid parking near main gate; campus is walkable once inside.

Museum & Tickets

  • Botanical Museum: Separate ticket (nominal, around ₹40–₹60 for adults depending on source).
  • Guided tours: Available for groups on request — check at the gate or info desk.
  • Confirm museum hours and closure days before planning (some sections closed on gazetted holidays).

Safety & Accessibility

Campus is generally flat and accessible; pathways are paved. Some interior museum rooms may have steps. Watch for vehicle movement on internal roads. Avoid restricted research areas and follow signboards.
