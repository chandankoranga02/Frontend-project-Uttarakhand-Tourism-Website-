Overview

Nanda Devi National Park, located in Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, is one of India’s most pristine high-altitude wilderness zones. It surrounds the towering Nanda Devi peak (7,816 m), the second highest mountain in India, and forms part of the larger Nanda Devi and Valley of Flowers National Parks UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The park remains largely untouched by human activity, preserving an ecosystem of alpine meadows, glacial valleys, and snow-covered ridges. Access is restricted to protect its delicate environment — only guided treks with limited permits are allowed from Lata or Tolma villages.

For adventurers and nature enthusiasts, Nanda Devi is a realm of raw beauty and silence. Its rugged terrain, wild rivers, and remote villages reflect the true grandeur of the Garhwal Himalayas.

Highlights

  • Home to Nanda Devi Peak (7,816 m).
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1988.
  • Rich biodiversity — Himalayan musk deer, snow leopard, and blue sheep.
  • Restricted eco-tourism zone — requires special permits.
  • Part of Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve.
Travel Tips:

Visitors need advance forest permits. Carry eco-friendly supplies only. Best explored May–October with local guides. Trekking routes may vary depending on wildlife regulations.
