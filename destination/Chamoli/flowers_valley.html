Overview

Valley of Flowers National Park is one of India’s most breathtaking natural wonders. Hidden in the Himalayas at an altitude of 3,600 meters, this lush valley bursts into a riot of colors every monsoon when over 500 species of wildflowers bloom together.

The valley was discovered in 1931 by British mountaineer Frank S. Smythe. Today, it stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and part of the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve. The trek to the valley is scenic, crossing rivers, wooden bridges, and misty trails.

From July to September, the entire landscape transforms into a vibrant carpet of color — with orchids, poppies, primulas, marigolds, and countless Himalayan flowers covering every inch of land.

Highlights

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2005.
  • Trek through alpine meadows and glacier streams.
  • Home to rare Himalayan flora & fauna — blue poppy, musk deer, snow leopard (rare sighting).
  • Picturesque Govindghat–Ghangaria trail (13 km trek).
  • Close to Hemkund Sahib (famous Sikh pilgrimage site).
Quick Tips:

Visit between July–September for peak bloom. No camping inside the park. Carry rain gear, water, and light snacks. Altitude gain is steep, so trek slowly.

Quick Facts

Altitude: 3,250 – 3,650 m

Best Season: July – September

Trek Distance: 13 km from Govindghat

Difficulty: Moderate

How to Reach

By Road: Drive from Rishikesh to Govindghat (270 km), then trek 13 km to Ghangaria.

Nearest Railway: Rishikesh (273 km)

Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant, Dehradun (295 km)
