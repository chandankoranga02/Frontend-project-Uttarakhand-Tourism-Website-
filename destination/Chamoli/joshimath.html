Overview

Joshimath, also known as Jyotirmath, is a sacred town and one of the four cardinal “Maths” established by Adi Guru Shankaracharya in the 8th century. Perched at 1,890 meters, it serves as the winter seat of Lord Badrinath and a key base for trekkers and pilgrims.

The town overlooks the confluence of Alaknanda and Dhauliganga rivers, surrounded by snow-capped peaks. It’s the last major settlement on the route to Badrinath and Auli, offering both spiritual solace and adventure access.

Joshimath also features cable car rides to Auli, ancient temples, and breathtaking viewpoints. Its tranquil vibe makes it a perfect blend of devotion and mountain serenity.

Highlights

  • Base point for Auli, Badrinath, and Valley of Flowers treks.
  • One of four sacred Maths of Adi Shankaracharya.
  • Hosts the winter idol of Lord Badrinath.
  • Asia’s longest ropeway to Auli (4.5 km).
  • Panoramic views of Nanda Devi and Hathi Parvat.
Quick Tips:

Best visited from October to March for snow views or May–June for treks. Book stays early during pilgrimage season. Carry winter clothing year-round.
