Overview

Hemkund Sahib, also known as Gurudwara Sri Hemkunt Sahib Ji, lies in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand at an altitude of 4,632 meters. Surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks, it is one of the highest Gurudwaras in the world, revered deeply by Sikhs and Hindus alike.

The lake beside the shrine — Hemkund (meaning “Lake of Snow”) — remains frozen for half the year. Pilgrims trek 6 km from Ghangaria through steep but scenic trails filled with alpine flowers, streams, and crisp mountain air. The entire setting feels divine and untouched.

The Gurudwara remains open only from June to early October due to extreme winter conditions. It’s a place where faith meets natural majesty — the reflection of the peaks in the lake is one of the most breathtaking sights in the Himalayas.

Highlights

  • Situated beside a glacial lake at 4,632 meters.
  • Dedicated to Guru Gobind Singh Ji’s meditation site.
  • Access via a scenic 6 km trek from Ghangaria.
  • Surrounded by seven majestic peaks.
  • Open for limited months (June–Oct).
Quick Tips:

Carry warm layers even in summer. Stay overnight at Ghangaria before starting the trek. Keep altitude sickness precautions. Maintain silence and respect near the Gurudwara premises.
