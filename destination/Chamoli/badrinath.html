Overview

Badrinath Temple, one of the holiest Hindu shrines, stands on the banks of the Alaknanda River in Chamoli district. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the temple is part of the revered Char Dham pilgrimage circuit and is visited by millions every year.

The colorful façade, intricate carvings, and the sacred idol of Lord Badrinarayan (carved from black stone) make this site spiritually powerful and visually magnificent. The surrounding snow-clad peaks add divine serenity to the entire valley.

At a height of 3,100 meters, the temple remains open from May to November due to heavy snowfall in winters. Pilgrims also visit nearby hot water springs, “Tapt Kund,” believed to have healing properties.

Highlights

  • Part of the Char Dham Yatra — one of India’s four sacred pilgrimage sites.
  • Surrounded by snow-covered peaks of Nar & Narayan ranges.
  • Tapt Kund hot spring just below the temple entrance.
  • Evening Aarti ceremony — spiritual experience not to miss.
  • Ideal base for nearby treks like Vasudhara Falls & Mana Village.
Quick Tips:

Visit between May–June or Sept–Oct for best weather. Carry warm clothing even in summer. Oxygen levels are lower — rest and hydrate well.
