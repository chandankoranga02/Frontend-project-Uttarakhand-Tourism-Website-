Bilkeshwar Mahadev Temple, Haridwar

Small, historic Shiva shrine located near Har Ki Pauri foot of Bilva Parvat — quick darshan spot with local religious significance.

Short Visit

Overview

Bilkeshwar Mahadev sits in the valley of Bilva Parvat close to Haridwar railway station and Har Ki Pauri. The small temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Parvati and is frequented by locals and pilgrims doing short darshan en route to larger ghat rituals. The temple is historically linked to local legends where Goddess Parvati worshipped Shiva. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The courtyard and approach are compact — expect a short darshan queue during mornings and festival days. There are small shops nearby for offerings and prasad. It’s ideal as a 20–45 minute stop when you’re exploring central Haridwar on foot.

Highlights

  • Close to Har Ki Pauri — easy to include in a walking route.
  • Peaceful small shrine with local religious stories and rituals.
  • Quick visit — good when exploring central Haridwar on foot.
Quick tips:

Wear modest clothing. Temple is small — avoid big bags during peak aarti hours. Carry small change for offerings.

Quick Facts

Distance: ~0.5–1 km from Haridwar Railway Station; walking distance to Har Ki Pauri. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Timings: Typically early morning to evening (local temple hours). Check locally on festivals. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Avg visit: 20–45 minutes.

How to Reach

From Haridwar Railway Station: Short walk (≈500 m). From Har Ki Pauri: walk or short auto ride.

Nearby

  • Har Ki Pauri (major ghat)
  • Bharat Mata Mandir (nearby)
  • Local shops & prasad stalls

Respect & Safety

Temples are busy during festivals — follow local queueing, avoid rush near ghat edges, and keep belongings secure.
