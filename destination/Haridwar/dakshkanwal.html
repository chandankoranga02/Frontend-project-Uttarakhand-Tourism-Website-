Daksha Mahadev Temple — Kankhal (Haridwar)

Ancient and mythic shrine linked to the Daksha Yajna legend — important pilgrimage site with a strong historical aura.

Overview

Daksha Mahadev Temple at Kankhal is associated with the legendary Daksha Yajna and Sati myth. The present temple complex has been rebuilt and restored multiple times; it is an important stop for pilgrims visiting Haridwar's historic Kankhal area. The complex hosts rituals, regular aartis and occasional large gatherings on festival days. There is typically no formal entry fee. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

The temple courtyard offers a close, devotional atmosphere — appropriate dress and respectful behaviour are expected. Kankhal is a cluster of temples and ashrams, so allow time to walk the narrow lanes and explore smaller shrines nearby.

Highlights

  • Strong mythological link to Daksha and Sati; historic rituals.
  • Compact, intense devotional feel — ideal for devotees and history seekers.
  • Combine with other Kankhal shrines for a short cultural loop.
Quick tips:

No entry fee normally; avoid visiting during very large festivals without planning. Many alleys are narrow — comfortable shoes and awareness of crowds are useful.

Quick Facts

Entry: No formal fee (check local notices). Location: Kankhal, ~6 km from central Haridwar. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Avg visit: 30–60 minutes depending on rituals.

How to Reach

From Haridwar: Short drive or local auto to Kankhal; walk between shrines.

Nearby

Other Kankhal shrines, ashrams and small shops. The area is compact and pedestrian-friendly.

Respect & Safety

Watch for narrow lanes, manage footwear and bags during crowded rituals, and follow local priest directions during ceremonies.
