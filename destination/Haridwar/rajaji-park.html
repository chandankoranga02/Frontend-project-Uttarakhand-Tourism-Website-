Overview

Rajaji National Park stretches across Dehradun, Haridwar and Pauri Garhwal districts — the Haridwar range (Chilla, Motichur sectors) is popular for short safaris from Haridwar and nearby towns. The terrain is a mix of sal forest, thorny scrub and riverine patches around the Ganga and its tributaries. Wildlife highlights include large elephant herds, sambar, barking deer, occasional leopard sightings and diverse resident and migratory birds.

Most visitors book morning/evening jeep or canter safaris with registered guides; sightings are always chance-based but early mornings improve success. Facilities at major ranges include small check-posts, basic rest points and designated parking. Bring water, binoculars and expect dusty trails during dry months.

Highlights

  • Jeep/canter safaris in Chilla & Motichur ranges — best early morning or late afternoon.
  • Large herds of elephants often visible in certain seasons.
  • Good birding: kingfishers, eagles, woodpeckers and winter migrants along riverine belts.
Quick tips:

Book safaris in advance for peak season; wear neutral clothing, carry insect repellent, and follow guide instructions. Road to some ranges is uneven — avoid high-clearance sedans on rough tracks.

Best time: Oct–Mar for pleasant weather and migrations.

Avg visit: Half-day (safari) to full day.

How to reach

From Haridwar: Chilla range ~30–45 mins drive depending on exact sector; local taxis and tour operators run transfers.

From Dehradun: ~1–2 hours depending on sector and traffic.

Booking

Book official safaris and avoid unregistered operators. Local forest department sites or park counters list timings and zone availability. Guides & vehicles can be hired at entry gates.

Safety & Notes

Do not feed wildlife, keep distance, follow guide instructions. Avoid visiting during extreme heat or heavy monsoon when trails can be closed.
