Chandi Devi Temple — Neel Parvat, Haridwar

Hilltop shrine reachable by ropeway and trek — offers panoramic city & Ganga views along with devotional darshan.

Overview

Chandi Devi Temple sits on Neel Parvat and is accessible by a ropeway (cable car) or by a walking trail. The temple is a popular pilgrim spot offering panoramic views over Haridwar and the Ganga. Typical darshan timings are early morning to evening; ropeway operates with scheduled hours. Many guide pages and ropeway services list operating hours roughly from ~7:00 AM to ~6:00 PM but check local notices for seasonal changes. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

The route up is scenic; the ropeway shortens travel time and is ideal for those who prefer not to trek. Festivals attract large crowds — plan early or late visits to avoid peak rush for comfortable darshan and better photos.

Highlights

  • Ropeway ride with great aerial views of Haridwar.
  • Scenic hilltop temple and excellent sunrise/sunset viewpoints.
  • Combine with Mansa Devi or Har Ki Pauri for a full-day pilgrimage circuit.
Quick tips:

If using ropeway, carry ID and small change for tickets; mornings are best for clearer views. Avoid heavy rains — ropeway operations may be suspended in bad weather.

Quick Facts

Ropeway & Darshan timings: Typical ropeway hours ~07:00–18:00; temple darshan ~6:00–20:00 (varies seasonally). Check local ropeway notice for exact hours. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Best time: Oct–Mar for crisp views.

Avg visit: 1–2 hours including ropeway/trek.

How to Reach

From Haridwar: Short drive to base station; ropeway or trekking options available.

Tickets

Ropeway charges apply (check counter / online booking). Temple entry generally free but special queues exist during festivals.

Safety

Ropeway may close in high winds/heavy rain. Trails can be uneven — choose ropeway if unsure about trekking fitness.
