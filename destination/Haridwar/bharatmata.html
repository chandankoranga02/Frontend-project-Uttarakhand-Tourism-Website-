Bharat Mata Mandir, Haridwar

Multi-storey Bharat Mata (Mother India) temple offering panoramic views and a compact museum of cultural exhibits — quick educational stop near the Ganga.

Overview

Bharat Mata Mandir in Haridwar is a multi-storey temple dedicated to Mother India with a series of floors representing maps, statues and cultural exhibits. It stands near the Ganga and is often visited for a short cultural/educational stop and for views over the river and city. The visit is short (30–45 minutes) and suitable for families and first-time visitors to Haridwar. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

The building’s floors show maps, reliefs and national symbolism — photo-friendly vantage points give a different perspective of the waterfront and nearby ghats. The temple is often combined with Har Ki Pauri and other nearby shrines during a walking tour of central Haridwar.

Highlights

  • Eight-storey structure with cultural displays.
  • Good short stop for panoramic city & river views.
  • Fast to visit — ideal for combining with other Haridwar sights.

How to Reach

Near central Haridwar — easily combined with Har Ki Pauri and marketplaces. Park & walk if coming by car.

Tips

Short queues; best to visit outside main aarti times if you want peaceful viewing and photos.

Booking

Usually no booking required; small donations sometimes accepted at the temple desk.
