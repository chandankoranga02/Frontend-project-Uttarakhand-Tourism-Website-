Overview

Munsiyari is a small hill station in Pithoragarh district, famous for panoramic views of the Himalayan range, including Panchachuli peaks. It serves as a base for trekking routes and glacier explorations in the region.

The town has a quaint vibe with local markets, orchards, and traditional Kumaoni architecture. River streams, terraced farms, and alpine forests surround the area, making it a serene escape for nature lovers.

Highlights

  • Snow-capped Himalayan views including Panchachuli peaks
  • Base for treks to Milam Glacier and Johar Valley
  • Local Kumaoni culture and handicrafts
  • Photography and birdwatching
  • Peaceful rivers and alpine landscapes
Visitor Tips:

Carry warm clothing even in summers. Start treks early in the day. Respect local culture and avoid littering. Best visiting months are Mar–Jun and Sep–Nov.
