Overview

Askot Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area in Pithoragarh district, spread across dense forests, alpine meadows, and river valleys. It is known for rare Himalayan wildlife including snow leopards, Himalayan tahr, musk deer, and a variety of birds.

The sanctuary offers trekking and nature trails through dense oak, pine, and rhododendron forests. Streams, waterfalls, and panoramic views make it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.

Highlights

  • Rare Himalayan wildlife sightings
  • Scenic trekking routes and nature trails
  • Birdwatching opportunities
  • Alpine meadows with seasonal flowers
  • Photography and eco-tourism experiences
Visitor Tips:

Carry binoculars and a camera. Trekking shoes are recommended. Respect wildlife and maintain distance. Early morning and late afternoons are best for spotting animals.

Quick Facts

Best Season: Mar–Jun & Sep–Nov

Trek Duration: Half-day to multi-day hikes

Entry Fee: Free; guides optional

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Pithoragarh (~60 km)

Road: Local transport or taxis to sanctuary base villages

Nearest Railhead: Kathgodam (~280 km)

Facilities

  • Local guides and trekking guides available
  • Basic resting spots and small eateries at base
  • Camping allowed with permissions
