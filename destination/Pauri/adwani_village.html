Overview

The Adwani Village trek is a moderate hike through pine and oak forests, terraced fields, and small streams. The trail leads to the picturesque village of Adwani, where visitors can observe traditional Garhwali lifestyle, wooden houses, and local farming practices.

The trek is suitable for beginners to moderate trekkers and takes around 2–3 hours one way. Villagers are friendly and often offer local tea, snacks, and guidance. The area is quiet and peaceful, ideal for nature walks, photography, and cultural immersion.

During monsoon, paths can be slippery, so trekking shoes are recommended. Best visited from October to May for clear skies and scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Highlights

  • Moderate trek through forests and streams
  • Traditional Garhwali village experience
  • Photography & bird watching opportunities
  • Local tea and snacks offered by villagers
  • Combine with Kandoliya Temple or Pauri sunset point
Quick tips:

Wear trekking shoes, carry water and light snacks. Early morning is best for clear views. Avoid trekking during heavy rains.
