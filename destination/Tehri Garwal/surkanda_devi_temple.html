Surkanda Devi Temple

Hilltop temple dedicated to Goddess Durga, offering panoramic Himalayan views and a short trek adventure.

Overview

Surkanda Devi Temple sits atop a hill near Dhanaulti and is known for its scenic trekking route and spiritual significance. The trek is moderate and surrounded by dense pine forests. Devotees visit during Navratri, and tourists enjoy the sunrise and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

The temple area has small shops selling prasad, souvenirs, and tea. Photography lovers often reach early to capture the valley view through misty mornings. Trek duration is roughly 2–3 hours round trip.

Visitors should wear comfortable shoes, carry water, and respect temple timings. Local guides are available for assistance if needed.

Highlights

  • Moderate trek through pine forests
  • Panoramic Himalayan views
  • Spiritual significance during Navratri
  • Tea & souvenir stalls on route
  • Combine with Dhanaulti Eco Park for a full day
Quick tips:

Best time: Sept–May. Trek boots recommended. Avoid heavy rains. Carry light snacks and water. Early morning trek avoids crowds.
