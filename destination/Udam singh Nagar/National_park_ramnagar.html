Overview

Jim Corbett National Park, located in Ramnagar, Uttarakhand, is a wildlife lover’s paradise. Established in 1936, it is India’s first national park and a renowned tiger reserve. Visitors can enjoy jeep safaris, birdwatching, and nature walks.

The park spans forests, grasslands, and riverine belts, providing a natural habitat for tigers, elephants, leopards, and numerous bird species. Guided tours enhance the experience and ensure safety while exploring wildlife.

Highlights

  • Tiger and elephant sightings
  • Jeep safaris and guided tours
  • Birdwatching & photography opportunities
  • Rivers, grasslands, and forest trekking trails
  • Nearby Ramnagar town facilities
Visitor Tips:

Book safaris in advance. Carry binoculars, camera, and water. Morning and evening are best for wildlife sightings. Follow park rules strictly.
