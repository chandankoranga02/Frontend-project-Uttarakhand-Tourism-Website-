Kedarnath Temple

One of the most sacred Hindu shrines, set in the majestic Garhwal Himalayas, with snow-clad peaks surrounding the holy temple.

Overview

Kedarnath Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage circuit. Located at an altitude of 3,583 meters, it is accessible via a trek of ~16 km from Gaurikund or by helicopter services from Phata or Guptkashi.

The temple’s architecture is ancient and simplistic, built with massive stones, reflecting Himalayan craftsmanship. Snow-capped peaks, glaciers, and the Mandakini River flowing nearby give it a serene and divine aura.

Visiting Kedarnath is not just spiritual but also an adventure due to the high-altitude trek, unpredictable weather, and rugged Himalayan terrain. Facilities are basic, with small dharamshalas and eateries around Gaurikund.

Highlights

  • Part of Char Dham Yatra (Shiva pilgrimage)
  • Situated at 3,583 m altitude
  • Surrounded by snow-clad Himalayan peaks
  • Mandakini river flows beside the temple
  • Accessible by trek or helicopter
Travel Tips:

Visit during May–October. Carry trekking shoes, warm clothing, and light essentials. Helicopter services available for those unable to trek. Respect the temple rules and timings.
