Overview

Dhari Devi Temple is considered a powerful spiritual site in Uttarakhand. Situated on the banks of the Alaknanda River, it is revered for its ancient idol and religious significance. Pilgrims believe that visiting the temple ensures protection during journeys, especially along the mountainous routes.

The temple structure is modest, blending with the surrounding landscape. The area is surrounded by lush greenery and Himalayan vistas, offering both devotion and nature experience. Limited facilities are available, mostly at nearby towns.

Access is possible via road up to the parking, followed by a short walk. The temple is especially busy during Navratri and other festivals, drawing devotees from across India.

Highlights

  • Ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Dhari Devi
  • Located along Alaknanda river banks
  • Short trekking access
  • Himalayan scenic surroundings
  • Festivals and pilgrimages attract devotees
Travel Tips:

Best visited from March to November. Wear comfortable shoes for walking. Respect temple timings and customs. Carry water and light snacks if trekking from the parking.
