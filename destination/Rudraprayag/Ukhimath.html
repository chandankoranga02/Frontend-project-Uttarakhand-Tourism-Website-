Ukhimath

A sacred town in Garhwal, Uttarakhand, Ukhimath serves as the winter seat of the Kedarnath deity and a spiritual hub for pilgrims.

Overview

Ukhimath is a small town in Rudraprayag district, known for its winter shrine of Kedarnath temple deity. Devotees visit the town during winter months when the Kedarnath shrine is closed due to snow.

The town is dotted with temples, offering a glimpse into Garhwal’s spiritual and architectural traditions. Surrounded by hills and rivers, Ukhimath provides serene landscapes and a peaceful atmosphere for visitors.

Facilities are basic but sufficient, with guesthouses, dhabas, and local markets catering to pilgrims. Accessibility is by road from Rudraprayag and nearby towns.

Highlights

  • Winter seat of Kedarnath deity
  • Garhwal temple architecture and culture
  • Peaceful town with scenic surroundings
  • Ideal for short spiritual visits
  • Local festivals and rituals attract devotees
Travel Tips:

Best visited March to November for comfortable weather. Respect local customs. Carry light woolens in winter. Photography allowed with consent near temples.
