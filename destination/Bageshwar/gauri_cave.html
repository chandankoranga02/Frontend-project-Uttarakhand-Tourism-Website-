Overview

Gauri Udiyar Cave Temple is a historic temple carved into rock cliffs and surrounded by forested hills. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Gauri and attracts devotees and hikers alike. A small stream runs nearby, creating a serene natural ambience perfect for meditation and photography.

The temple is accessible by a short uphill trek, with well-laid stone steps. Local legends highlight the cave as an ancient meditation site for sages. Small markets at the base provide refreshments and souvenirs for visitors.

Highlights

  • Historic rock-cut temple dedicated to Goddess Gauri
  • Scenic forest trails with minimal crowd
  • Stream and natural springs nearby
  • Photography and light hiking
  • Local folklore and cultural experience
Visitor Tips:

Wear comfortable walking shoes. Early mornings are peaceful and less crowded. Respect the temple traditions, avoid loud music, and carry water and snacks. Avoid visiting during heavy rains due to slippery steps.

Quick Facts

How to Reach

Nearest Town: Pithoragarh (~40 km)

Road: Motorable road till base, then 15–20 min uphill walk

Nearest Railhead: Kathgodam (~250 km)

Facilities

  • Small tea stalls at base
  • Rest benches along the steps
  • Local guides for history & folklore
