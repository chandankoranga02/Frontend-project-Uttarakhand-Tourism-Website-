Pindari Glacier Trek

Classic Himalayan glacier trek in Kumaon — scenic moraines, high-altitude camps, and dramatic views of Nanda Devi and other peaks.

Overview

The Pindari Glacier trek (base: Khati/Pindari) is a popular 6–8 day out-and-back trek in the Kumaon Himalaya. The trail follows the Pindari River valley, passes lush meadows, rhododendron and oak forests, and ends at the glacier snout offering close views of towering peaks—Nanda Kot (6,861 m), Maiktoli and Panwali Dwar among them.

Compared with high technical glacier routes, Pindari is non-technical but requires good fitness. Camps are typically set at Dwali, Phurkia, Khati and the glacier base. The route is scenic year-round when open (May–October), with post-monsoon clarity giving the best mountain vistas.

Highlights

  • Stunning view of Nanda Kot, Maiktoli & Panwali Dwar
  • Accessible glacier snout — excellent for beginners with experience
  • Lush alpine meadows, forested trails and river crossings
  • Base villages (Dhakuri, Khati) give authentic Kumaoni experience
  • Good for photography, star-gazing and seasonal wildflowers
Practical tips:

Fitness: moderate trek fitness (6–8 hrs walking some days). Pack: warm layers, waterproof jacket, trekking boots, first-aid, sunscreen, headlamp. Permit: register with local forest/trek office if required. Altitude: max ~3,600–3,700 m at glacier snout — acclimatise gradually. Weather: monsoon makes trail slippery; best months May–June and Sept–Oct.

Route & Typical Itinerary

  • Day 0: Kathgodam → Bageshwar / Karanprayag (drive)
  • Day 1: Drive to Dhakuri (trek start) — camp
  • Day 2: Dhakuri → Khati / Phurkia — camp
  • Day 3: Khati → Dwali/Phurkia — camp
  • Day 4: Dwali → Pindari Glacier snout & return to camp
  • Day 5–6: Return trek to Dhakuri and drive back

Safety & Responsibility

Respect local environment: carry out all plastic, follow campfire rules, and use local guides/porters to support village economy. Glacier travel can present crevasses and unstable moraine — stick to marked routes and experienced leaders. If weather turns bad, descend immediately.

Quick Facts

Difficulty: Moderate (non-technical)

Max Altitude: ~3,600–3,700 m (glacier snout)

Typical Duration: 5–7 days (round trip)

How to Reach

Road: Drive to Dhakuri/Deval via Bageshwar or Karanprayag. Kathgodam is nearest railhead (base for Kumaon treks).

Nearest Railhead: Kathgodam (~220 km drive depending route)

Nearest Airport: Pantnagar (~250 km)

Local Support

  • Guides and porters available at Dhakuri/Khati
  • Basic tea-huts and seasonal camps on route
  • Best to pre-book through a local operator for tents & meals
