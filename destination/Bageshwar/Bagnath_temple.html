Overview

Bagnath Temple is a revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, situated at the confluence of the Gomati and Saryu rivers in Bageshwar district. Built in the 10th century by the Chand rulers, the temple is a masterpiece of ancient Kumaoni stone architecture.

“Bagnath” literally means “Tiger Lord,” symbolizing Lord Shiva. The temple complex contains multiple smaller shrines, stone idols, and inscriptions dating back centuries. It remains the focal point of the annual **Shivratri fair**, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists.

Visiting Bagnath Temple offers not only spiritual peace but also a glimpse of Kumaon’s medieval craftsmanship and devotional heritage.

Highlights

  • 10th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva
  • Situated at the sacred confluence of Saryu and Gomati rivers
  • Annual Shivratri Mela draws huge crowds
  • Ancient stone carvings and idols
  • Stunning sunrise and river views around the temple ghat
Visitor Tips:

Footwear not allowed inside temple premises. Visit early morning for peaceful darshan. Photography may be restricted in sanctum area — check local notices.
