Gangotri Temple

One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, dedicated to Goddess Ganga, located in Uttarkashi district along the banks of Bhagirathi River.

Overview

Gangotri Temple is a sacred shrine in the Himalayas, marking the origin of the holy Ganges River. Pilgrims visit to offer prayers to Goddess Ganga and perform rituals along the riverbank. The temple has a distinct architecture with stone and wood carvings, and the serene surroundings add to the spiritual atmosphere.

The temple area is surrounded by snow-capped peaks and coniferous forests. Access is primarily by road from Uttarkashi, with limited facilities for lodging and food. Best visited during summer months when roads are accessible and weather is mild.

Visitors often combine Gangotri with other Char Dham destinations like Yamunotri and Kedarnath for a spiritual circuit in Uttarakhand.

Highlights

  • Part of Char Dham pilgrimage
  • Origin of River Ganga
  • Beautiful Himalayan backdrop
  • Festivals: Ganga Dussehra and others
  • Short trekking options nearby
Travel Tips:

Best visited May to June or Sept to Oct. Carry warm clothing, comfortable shoes, water, and snacks. Respect local customs and temple timings. Avoid visiting in heavy snowfall months.
