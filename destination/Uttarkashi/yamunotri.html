Yamunotri Temple

The sacred temple of Goddess Yamuna, located at the origin of the Yamuna River, in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand.

Overview

Yamunotri Temple is a revered shrine dedicated to Goddess Yamuna. Situated amidst high mountains and hot water springs, the temple sees devotees undertaking a challenging trek or pony ride from Janki Chatti. Pilgrims perform rituals and offer prayers at the temple and at nearby hot springs.

The temple has a simple yet spiritual architecture, blending seamlessly with the natural surroundings. The trek offers beautiful Himalayan vistas, snow-capped peaks in winter, and alpine meadows in summer.

The temple is part of the Char Dham Yatra and is visited along with Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.

Highlights

  • Origin of River Yamuna
  • Hot water springs near temple
  • Challenging yet scenic trek from Janki Chatti
  • Spiritual rituals and pilgrimages
  • Best visited in summer months
Travel Tips:

Best visited May – June or Sept – Oct. Carry trekking shoes, warm clothing, water, and snacks. Avoid heavy snowfall months. Respect local customs and rituals.
