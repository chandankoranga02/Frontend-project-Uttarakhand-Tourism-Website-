Ramgarh (The Fruit Bowl of Kumaon)

Peaceful hill town near Nainital, famous for its orchards and Himalayan views.

About Ramgarh

Nestled at an altitude of around 1789 meters in Nainital district, Ramgarh is known as the “Fruit Bowl of Kumaon” due to its rich orchards of apples, plums, peaches, and apricots. This quiet hamlet offers majestic views of snow-clad Himalayan peaks and serene walking trails. It was once home to writers like Rabindranath Tagore and Mahadevi Verma.

Travel Information

Nearest Bus Stand: Nainital (25 km)

Nearest Railway Station: Kathgodam (45 km)

Best Time to Visit: March to June & September to November

Average Cost per Person: ₹1000 – ₹2000 (Stay + Food)

Time to Explore: 1 full day

Places to Stay & Eat

There are several cozy homestays and boutique resorts such as Rhododendron Retreat and Neemrana’s Ramgarh Bungalows. For food, you’ll find cafes serving local and continental dishes, with scenic outdoor seating options.

Nearby Attractions

  • Mukteshwar (25 km) – for temples and adventure sports
  • Bhimtal (30 km) – famous for its lake and boating
  • Nainital (25 km) – main hill station nearby
